About The Iron Yard

The Iron Yard teaches intensive, three-month courses that mentor students into professional junior-level programmers. Our program goes far beyond tactical skills and teaches students to think like a software engineer. Students have spent the past twelve weeks studying Mobile Engineering, Front End Engineering, and Rails Engineering and today they will present their final projects, which showcase the skills they have learned.

Interested in hiring a graduate?

All of our graduates are ready for their first jobs as junior developers. If you meet someone who would be a great fit for your team, please feel free to reach out to them directly. Graduates' contact info can be found here. If you have questions or want more information about how our job placement program works, get in touch with the Houston team at houston@theironyard.com.

Interested in taking a class or learning more?

Our next classes begin September 26, 2016. We are accepting applications now. Applications can be found on our website: theironyard.com/apply. We also offer free classes for kids and host a variety of meet-ups and free events. For more information on any of these programs, visit our website or email houston@theironyard.com.

Magic: The Gathering has a worldwide user base in the millions and has been around for 20 years. This leads to trading and speculating, and as some of these cards are worth any where from a couple of bucks to hundreds and in some rare cases thousands of dollars. This leads to an element of trust between players looking for trades of their high end cards and this need is usually fulfilled by going to a local card shop that the traders trust. This is where Good Wolf Trading comes in, using a Magic API and backend database, GWT aims to provide users with a online trading experience that can be used to facilitate the trading of cards locally after trust has been established, and providing a view of the local trading pool prior to that.
