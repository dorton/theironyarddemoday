About The Iron Yard

The Iron Yard teaches intensive, three-month courses that mentor students into professional junior-level programmers. Our program goes far beyond tactical skills and teaches students to think like a software engineer. Students have spent the past twelve weeks studying Front End Engineering and Rails Engineering and today they will present their final projects, which showcase the skills they have learned.

ZAKI AHMED

SAVOR IT
https://savorit.herokuapp.com/

Zaki Ahmed, graduated from the University of Houston class of 2014 with a BS in Petroleum Engineering. Having problem solving and analytical skills he decided to try his hand at coding due to the similar nature after deciding to do a career change from oil & gas.

People love 2 things in the world, food and impulse shopping. Combining that with a social media aspect and you have SavorIt. Savorit is a social media app with the basic idea of your friends posting food that they have eaten at certain restaurants in your local area. The main distinction between this and instagram is that all of these food products in your local area can be bought and delivered straight from the SavorIt application. Thus feeding all of your food cravings and also giving you ideas of what you want to eat instead of going to Yelp, searching through a ton of random restaurants and deciding what you want after looking at their menu. The pictures give users a mental stimulation of what you actually want to eat and the capability to buy whatever food you want that your friends are showing you.

DOUGLAS DRAHEM

http://alpineapp.herokuapp.com/

When oil prices plummeted to $30.00 per barrel early in 2015 Douglas found himself amongst the ranks of the unemployed as energy related companies were forced to trim their budgets. Now, 12 weeks later he has developed his first web based application, Alpine.

Alpine takes its inspiration from the building murals that are found in Alpine, Texas. This application provides an approach to “shoeboxing” an image for safe keeping. Alpine provides a method to catalog, reference and display building art I find and keep the images and locations documented for later reference. An image can be captured and transferred to the application with the time and location data extracted from the image data. Users can vote on their favorite murals and view them on a map, helping the daily commuter experience a hidden world that would perhaps otherwise not be seen.
