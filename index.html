LEROY GARDNER

LIFTWHEN
liftwhen.herokuapp.com

LiftWhen - A Focus On The Platform: State of the industry in the administration and running of an Olympic Weightlifting competition involves index cards and Microsoft Excel. Broken. My application moves this administration, record keeping and information display from the table top with index cards to modernity. We allow the admin to focus on saving time, data integrity and communication. We allow coaches to focus on coaching not counting. We help fans cheer by enabling ease of information reporting. Finally we help athletes focus on lifting. We bring the sport back to a focus on the performance on the platform. I developed this application using my experience as a certified USA Weightlifting coach and competitor. It was painful as coach counting reps, competitors and estimating timelines for my athletes performance. Information was hard to come by. It was painful helping meet organizers enter results which were hand written on an index card into a spreadsheet. Results were slow to be publish, because of faxes, snail mailing and emails. Payment was painful because checks. In short we make it easier for meet organizers to run a successful meet, and report results.

SPENCER NORMAN

MY FISHING RECORDS
myfishingrecords.co

A lot of anglers like to keep a log of their fishing trips for reference. I couldn’t find a decent application that made this experience very helpful or easy. So, I decided to make this a digital process and one that would help me on my future fishing trips. Fish respond to weather conditions in different ways. A lot of people have theories on what they do but I wanted to personalize it. A major part of this application allows you log the previous 4 days of weather leading up to the day that you went fishing and the day of that trip. The application then allows you to filter your trips based on the same information for an upcoming trip. This allows you to see how, individually, you fish best in those conditions. You can also record the water conditions and add your own notes to the trips so that you can easily reference these items.

HTML5, SCSS, JAVASCRIPT, JQUERY, UNDERSCORE.JS, BOOTSTRAP, FORECAST.IO API, NAVIONICS API, RUBY ON RAILS, DEVISE, GEOCODER, AWS, ACTIVE MODEL, POSTGRES

MICHAEL PADON

MAKERBOARD
makerboard.co

The Maker movement is empowering people worldwide to go out and make things, from websites to tables to jewelry. It's awesome, but these makers sometimes lack the equipment required to make the cool things they want to make. Enter the Makerspace: a warehouse of equipment that members pay a monthly fee to use. These spaces are hubs for full-time artists, part-time hobbyists, and general enthusiasts that like to buy local, handmade stuff. Makerspaces can enable makers to actually make money off their craft, but it's often hard for a consumer, who wants to hire or buy from a local maker, to figure out who the Makerspace members are and what they are capable of creating. The current system is a hodgepodge of member databases, spreadsheets, and business cards on a table. It's hard to point an interested client in the direction of the right maker. In comes MakerBoard. It's an online board for Makerspaces where members can post their profile and customers can get in contact with the right person for their job. It takes all of the guess work out of finding capable makers for jobs.

HTML5, CSS3, SASS, JAVASCRIPT, REACT, JSNOX (INSTEAD OF JSX), PARSE (AND PARSE CLOUD CODE), STRIPE CREDIT CARD PROCESSING, BACKBONE, MOCHA, CHAI, BOWER, NPM, LOADER, AND JQUERY
